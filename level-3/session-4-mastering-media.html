Phase 1: Context & Importance

Time Allocation: 15% of Session Time

The "Work-Ready" Objective

To deliver a structured, impactful 90-second message in a high-stakes audio-only interview format, ensuring clarity and resonance under pressure.

Commercial Relevance Script

In today's interconnected landscape, your executive voice is a critical asset. Media appearances, particularly podcasts, offer direct access to key stakeholders, industry peers, and potential clients. The ability to articulate your vision, defend your position, or introduce a new initiative concisely and compellingly in an audio-first environment directly impacts brand reputation, market influence, and investor confidence. Failing to master this means ceding narrative control and missing strategic opportunities.

Phase 2: Baseline Calibration

Time Allocation: 15% of Session Time

The Scenario Setup

You are the CEO of a rapidly growing tech startup that just announced a major acquisition. You have been invited for a 15-minute segment on 'Future Forward Insights', a leading industry podcast, to discuss the strategic rationale and future implications. The host is known for direct, probing questions. Your goal is to articulate the acquisition's value proposition and address potential market skepticism within a 90-second opening statement.

The Input Baseline (The Fallback)

Most executives, when asked to speak on their company's strategy in a media context, tend to ramble. They provide too much background, use internal jargon, or fail to get to the core message quickly. Their initial response often lacks a clear, repeatable structure, resulting in a diluted impact and lost opportunity to control the narrative.

Phase 3: Technical Demonstration

Time Allocation: 10% of Session Time

Rule 1

The 'Headline-Bridge-Impact' Structure: Start with a concise, compelling headline statement of your core message. Immediately bridge it to the 'why now' or 'what changed' context. Conclude with the tangible, future-oriented impact for the audience or market.

Rule 2

The 'Three-Point Expansion': After the initial Headline-Bridge-Impact, be prepared to expand on precisely three key supporting points, each introduced with a clear signpost. This creates a logical, digestible flow for the listener and prevents tangents.

Coach Script (The Target Output)

Host: 'Welcome, CEO. Your recent acquisition is big news. What's the immediate takeaway for our listeners?' Coach (as student): 'This acquisition immediately positions us to dominate the AI-driven analytics market. We recognized a critical convergence point between predictive modeling and real-time data streams, and this move allows us to deliver unparalleled, actionable intelligence to enterprises globally, unlocking billions in efficiency gains. Specifically, we're targeting: one, enhanced predictive accuracy; two, seamless integration capabilities; and three, a significantly expanded global market reach. This isn't just growth; it's a strategic leap in market leadership.'

Phase 4: Deliberate Practice & Peer Feedback Loops

Time Allocation: 60% of Session Time

The Global Speaker Bar Success Metrics — to pass, the student must flawlessly demonstrate all three tracking pillars simultaneously.

  1. Technical Accuracy: The response must explicitly contain: 1. A clear 'Headline' statement. 2. A 'Bridge' connecting to context or 'why now'. 3. A 'Future-Oriented Impact' statement. 4. A distinct, verbally signposted 'Three-Point Expansion' (e.g., 'First, we will...', 'Second, this enables...', 'Third, our focus is...'). Failure to include any of these structural elements results in a 'Fail'.
  2. Linguistic Fluency / Bimodal Alignment: Physical Stage: Maintain consistent eye contact with the host, controlled hand gestures (if any) that support the message, and an open, confident posture. Virtual Frame: Maintain direct lens-contact for 80% of the response, ensure microphone resonance is clear and consistent (no peaking/muffling), and frame is stable. Speech must be fluid, articulate, and free of verbal tics.
  3. Executive Presence: Zero filler words (uh, um, like, you know). Consistent, measured vocal resonance and pace. Project authority and conviction without arrogance. Maintain composure under perceived pressure. No vocal fry or upward inflection at sentence ends. Speak with definitive statements, not questions.

The Core Drill

The Podcast Sprint: Each student will assume the role of an executive being interviewed on 'Future Forward Insights'. The coach or a peer will act as the host, delivering a pre-scripted, open-ended prompt related to a recent significant business event (e.g., major product launch, critical market shift, unexpected quarterly results). The student has 90 seconds to deliver their structured response using the 'Headline-Bridge-Impact' and 'Three-Point Expansion' rules. After each 90-second sprint, the student receives immediate peer and coach feedback against the Global Speaker Bar metrics.

The Rotational Execution Guidelines

Students rotate roles: Speaker, Host, and Feedback Provider. The Host delivers the prompt. The Speaker executes the 90-second structured response. The Feedback Provider uses a checklist to assess Technical Accuracy, Linguistic Fluency/Bimodal Alignment, and Executive Presence. Each round consists of one sprint and 2 minutes of structured feedback. Three full rotations ensure each student experiences all roles. Timing is strict: 90 seconds for response, 60 seconds for peer feedback, 60 seconds for coach feedback.

Phase 5: The Complexity Shift (Conditional Trigger)

Stress-Test 1 (Time Crunch)

The host interrupts at 60 seconds, stating, 'We're short on time, can you summarize your final two points in 10 seconds?' The student must condense and deliver the remaining key points instantly and concisely.

Stress-Test 2 (Adversarial)

During the 'Three-Point Expansion', the host interjects with a skeptical or challenging question: 'Many analysts believe this acquisition is overpriced. How do you respond to concerns about shareholder value?' The student must pivot, acknowledge the challenge, and seamlessly integrate a brief, confident rebuttal before returning to their structured points.

Time Allocation: Remaining Session Margin

Phase 7: Executive Closing

Time Allocation: Final 2-3 Minutes

Coach Coaching Notes & Jargon Check

Key Phrase to Use

Own the narrative, or someone else will.

Common Pitfall to Ban

The 'wandering preamble' – starting without a clear, concise headline.

Monday Morning Summary

Takeaway 1 (Verbal)

Every media appearance requires a pre-defined 'Headline-Bridge-Impact' structure, followed by a 'Three-Point Expansion' to ensure your core message is delivered and supported under any circumstance.

Takeaway 2 (Delivery)

In both physical and virtual settings, direct eye (or lens) contact, a steady vocal resonance, and the elimination of filler words project unwavering executive presence and control.

The Golden Rule

Your message must be immediately clear, demonstrably relevant, and structurally repeatable.
