Phase 1: Context & Importance

Time Allocation: 15% of Session Time

The "Work-Ready" Objective

Defend non-negotiable ethical standards in high-stakes negotiations without compromising deal viability.

Commercial Relevance Script

In today's global market, ethical breaches carry exponential financial and reputational costs. Your ability to articulate and defend your organization's core values, particularly in contentious negotiations, is not merely a moral imperative—it's a strategic asset. This session equips you to prevent value erosion and maintain long-term stakeholder trust when faced with pressure to compromise.

Phase 2: Baseline Calibration

Time Allocation: 15% of Session Time

The Scenario Setup

You are a Senior Business Development Manager for a pharmaceutical company. You are in the final stages of negotiating a critical supply chain contract with a new third-party logistics provider. During the final review, the provider subtly suggests a 'fast-track' customs clearance process, implying a grey area payment to local officials, which would significantly reduce your shipping times and costs but directly violates your company's anti-corruption policy.

The Input Baseline (The Fallback)

Your initial response: 'I'm not sure about that. Our policy is pretty strict. Can we look for other options?' (Hesitant, reactive, lacks firm structural defense).

Phase 3: Technical Demonstration

Time Allocation: 10% of Session Time

Rule 1

State the non-negotiable principle immediately and unambiguously. Use phrases like 'Our policy is clear,' or 'This is non-negotiable for us.'

Rule 2

Reframe the discussion by offering a compliant alternative or focusing on shared legitimate goals. Pivot to compliant alternatives or reinforce shared legitimate business objectives to steer the conversation away from the unethical proposal without shutting down the negotiation entirely.

Coach Script (The Target Output)

Our policy is unequivocally clear on this matter: we do not engage in any practices that could be construed as bribery or unethical payments. This is a non-negotiable standard for our organization. We are committed to a fully compliant and transparent supply chain, and I am confident we can achieve our mutual objectives within those parameters. Let's focus on optimizing the legitimate aspects of this process, perhaps by reviewing alternative routing or documentation strategies, to ensure timely delivery while upholding our integrity.

Phase 4: Deliberate Practice & Peer Feedback Loops

Time Allocation: 60% of Session Time

The Global Speaker Bar Success Metrics — to pass, the student must flawlessly demonstrate all three tracking pillars simultaneously.

  1. Technical Accuracy: Student must use the exact two-part structure: 1. Clear, unambiguous statement of non-negotiable ethical principle. 2. Immediate pivot to compliant alternatives or legitimate shared objectives. No deviation from the prescribed formula.
  2. Linguistic Fluency / Bimodal Alignment: Physical Stage: Maintain direct, open posture. No fidgeting. Use deliberate hand gestures to emphasize non-negotiable points. Virtual Frame: Direct lens contact when stating the principle. Stable, resonant vocal tone. Ensure microphone picks up the firm, unwavering delivery. Avoid looking away or down.
  3. Executive Presence: Zero filler words (um, ah, like). Consistent, confident vocal resonance throughout the statement. Maintain a composed, unruffled demeanor. Project authority, not aggression. Deliver the message with conviction and strategic calm.

Students will rotate in pairs. One student acts as the Senior Business Development Manager, the other as the Third-Party Logistics Provider applying pressure.
