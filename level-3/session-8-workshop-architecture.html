Phase 1: Context & Importance

The "Work-Ready" Objective

To design and articulate a structured, impactful 90-minute training module that drives specific, measurable skill transfer within an organization, leveraging bimodal delivery.

Commercial Relevance Script

Effective internal training is not a cost, it's an investment in scalable expertise and operational efficiency. Unstructured knowledge transfer wastes resources and dilutes impact. Mastering workshop architecture ensures your insights translate directly into actionable skills for your teams, accelerating project timelines, reducing errors, and solidifying your role as a strategic enabler. This session equips you to build training that delivers tangible business outcomes, not just information.

Phase 2: Baseline Calibration

Time Allocation: 15% of Session Time

The Scenario Setup

You are a Senior Project Manager tasked with upskilling your team on a new agile methodology. You have 90 minutes allocated to deliver a critical segment of a full-day training. Your goal is to ensure your team can immediately apply the core principles.

The Input Baseline (The Fallback)

Most will approach this by listing topics, creating slides, and lecturing through the content. They might include a Q&A or a simple group discussion at the end, assuming information delivery equals skill transfer. There's often no explicit structure for practice, bimodal adaptation, or measurable outcomes.

Phase 3: Technical Demonstration

Time Allocation: 10% of Session Time

Rule 1

The 80/20 Practice Rule: Structure your 90-minute module with 80% dedicated to a high-stakes, repeatable drill and 20% to concise context and debrief. Every phase must explicitly define an action, not just a topic.

Rule 2

Bimodal Integration Mandate: For every drill and instruction, specify distinct execution mechanics for both Physical Stage (e.g., spatial arrangement, whiteboard use) and Virtual Frame (e.g., screen share, breakout rooms, chat functions). This ensures immediate adaptability.

Coach Script (The Target Output)

Alright team, here's how we architect a 90-minute module for 'Advanced Risk Mitigation in Q3 Projects'. We start with a 10-minute 'Work-Ready Objective' and 'Commercial Relevance' – why this matters NOW. Then, 10 minutes for 'Baseline Calibration' – what are their current assumptions? Next, a 5-minute 'Technical Demonstration' of the new risk matrix. Then, the core: 60 minutes for 'Deliberate Practice'. Here, they'll apply the matrix to a live project scenario, in pairs, using a shared digital template if virtual, or physical whiteboards if in-person. We'll rotate through three mini-drills, each 15 minutes, followed by 5 minutes of peer feedback. Finally, 5 minutes for 'Validation & Reflection' and a 2-minute 'Executive Closing' with clear next steps. Notice the 80/20 split on practice, and how we've already considered both physical and virtual delivery for each segment.

Phase 4: Deliberate Practice & Peer Feedback Loops

Time Allocation: 60% of Session Time

The Global Speaker Bar Success Metrics — to pass, the student must flawlessly demonstrate all three tracking pillars simultaneously.

  1. Technical Accuracy: The blueprint must adhere precisely to the 80/20 Rule for practice and clearly define each phase with actionable content, not just topics. All instructions for the proposed module's activities must be specific and unambiguous.
  2. Linguistic Fluency / Bimodal Alignment: The presentation of the blueprint must be articulate, concise, and devoid of jargon. Students must explicitly state how each major phase of their proposed module would be executed differently, yet effectively, for both a Physical Stage (e.g., room layout, physical aids) and a Virtual Frame (e.g., platform features, digital tools, camera engagement).
  3. Executive Presence: The student must present their blueprint with unwavering confidence, maintaining direct eye contact (or lens contact), a steady vocal resonance, and eliminating all filler words. Their rationale for each design choice must be clear and persuasive, demonstrating leadership in curriculum design.

The Core Drill

The Training Blueprint: Students will design a 90-minute internal skill-transfer module, selecting a specific, high-priority skill relevant to their organization. The blueprint must explicitly detail: 1. A work-ready objective. 2. A commercial relevance statement. 3. A baseline calibration prompt. 4. Two technical transformation rules. 5. A 60-minute core drill with bimodal execution mechanics. 6. Defined success metrics for technical accuracy, bimodal alignment, and executive presence for their proposed module.

The Rotational Execution Guidelines

Students will work in pairs. Each student will present their 90-minute module blueprint to their partner in a 5-minute pitch, articulating the structure, content, and bimodal mechanics. The partner provides direct, actionable feedback against the three Global Speaker Bar pillars. Roles reverse. This cycle repeats twice, with refinement after each feedback round. The coach observes and interjects for targeted corrections on structural integrity and bimodal specificity.

Phase 5: The Complexity Shift (Conditional Trigger)

Stress-Test 1 (Time Crunch)

Reduce your 90-minute module to 45 minutes without sacrificing the core skill transfer. Identify the absolute essentials and justify every cut, maintaining the 80/20 practice rule.

Stress-Test 2 (Adversarial)

Present your 90-minute blueprint to a simulated 'CFO' who questions the ROI of internal training, demanding a direct correlation between your module's structure and quantifiable business impact. Defend your architecture and its inherent value proposition.
