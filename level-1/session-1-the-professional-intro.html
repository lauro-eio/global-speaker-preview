Phase 1: Context & Importance

Time Allocation: 15% of Session Time

The "Work-Ready" Objective

To consistently deliver a concise, impactful self-introduction that immediately establishes credibility and relevance in any professional setting, securing the next conversation or opportunity.

Commercial Relevance Script

Your first 60 seconds determine whether you earn a second 60 seconds. In high-stakes environments like client pitches, investor meetings, or critical networking events, a weak introduction is a direct financial liability. It costs you deals, access, and influence. Mastering this foundational skill ensures you convert initial contact into tangible progress, protecting your time and maximizing every interaction.

Phase 2: Baseline Calibration

Time Allocation: 15% of Session Time

The Scenario Setup

You are at an industry networking event or a virtual 'meet the team' session. You have approximately 60 seconds to introduce yourself to a key decision-maker or potential collaborator you are meeting for the first time, who knows nothing about you.

The Input Baseline (The Fallback)

A typical, unstructured introduction often sounds like: 'Hi, I'm [Name]. I work at [Company] as a [Role]. We do a lot of interesting things in [Industry]. I've been there for [X] years. It's really busy. What do you do?' This approach lacks a clear value proposition, fails to establish immediate relevance, and often ends with the speaker trailing off, leaving the listener unengaged.

Phase 3: Technical Demonstration

Time Allocation: 10% of Session Time

Rule 1

The 'Who-What-Why' Structure: Immediately state your Name, your current Role/Company, and one specific, quantifiable impact or value you deliver. This establishes your professional identity and core contribution within the first 15-20 seconds.

Rule 2

The 'Future-Forward' Hook: Conclude with a clear, concise statement that articulates your current focus, a problem you are solving, or a specific area of expertise you are seeking to engage on. This provides a natural bridge for the listener to ask a relevant follow-up question.

Coach Script (The Target Output)

Good morning. I'm [Coach Name], a Senior Consultant at Global Dynamics, where I lead high-impact operational efficiency projects, typically reducing client overhead by 15-20% in the first six months. Currently, I'm focused on developing scalable solutions for integrating AI into legacy financial systems to drive predictive analytics. I look forward to discussing how these advancements can specifically benefit your sector.

Phase 4: Deliberate Practice & Peer Feedback Loops

Time Allocation: 60% of Session Time

The Global Speaker Bar Success Metrics — to pass, the student must flawlessly demonstrate all three tracking pillars simultaneously.

  1. Technical Accuracy: Strict adherence to the 'Who-What-Why' Structure and the 'Future-Forward' Hook. Every element must be present, in order, and delivered within the 60-second limit. Any deviation from the prescribed verbal framework results in a fail.
  2. Linguistic Fluency / Bimodal Alignment: Physical Stage: Confident posture, open body language, direct eye contact maintained for 70-80% of the delivery, clear vocal projection without shouting. Virtual Frame: Consistent direct lens contact, stable head and shoulder framing, clear audio with no background noise, vocal resonance optimized for microphone, no distracting gestures.
  3. Executive Presence: Zero filler words (um, ah, like, you know). Steady, controlled vocal pace. Confident, unwavering tone. No apologies or self-deprecating language. Project authority and competence from start to finish. The message must convey clarity and conviction.

The Core Drill

The 60-Second Hook

The Rotational Execution Guidelines

Students will pair up. One student delivers their 60-Second Hook, timed precisely. The partner provides immediate, structured feedback focusing on Technical Accuracy first. Then, roles are reversed. After two rounds, pairs rotate. Repeat this cycle three times. Each student must deliver their Hook at least three times, receiving feedback each round. Physical participants will stand and make eye contact. Virtual participants must maintain direct lens contact and optimal microphone resonance.

Phase 5: The Complexity Shift (Conditional Trigger)

Stress-Test 1 (Time Crunch)

Reduce the 60-Second Hook to a 30-Second Micro-Hook. Condense your 'Who-What-Why' and 'Future-Forward' to their absolute essence, maintaining impact and clarity. The goal is to deliver the core message with even greater precision under severe time constraints.

Stress-Test 2 (Adversarial)

Deliver your 60-Second Hook to a partner instructed to show disinterest (e.g., checking phone, looking away, sighing). Maintain unwavering confidence, vocal resonance, and direct engagement (physical eye contact or virtual lens contact) without altering your pace or content. The objective is to command attention despite external distraction.

Phase 6: Validation & Reflection

Time Allocation: Remaining Session Margin

The Gains Check-In Prompt

How will you integrate the 'Who-What-Why' structure and 'Future-Forward' hook into your next critical professional interaction, whether in person or online?
