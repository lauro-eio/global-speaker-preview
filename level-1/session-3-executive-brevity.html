Phase 1: Context & Importance

Time Allocation: 15% of Session Time

The "Work-Ready" Objective

To consistently deliver high-impact, actionable updates or proposals in 60 seconds or less, ensuring critical information is conveyed immediately.

Commercial Relevance Script

In today's high-velocity business environment, time is a non-renewable asset. Executives operate under constant pressure, making decisions based on concise, relevant information. Your ability to deliver the Bottom Line Up Front – to respect their time by immediately stating your core message – is not merely a courtesy; it is a strategic imperative. It establishes you as a credible, efficient communicator, accelerates decision cycles, and positions you as a valuable contributor. Failing to do so wastes executive time, dilutes your message, and signals a lack of strategic thinking.

Phase 2: Baseline Calibration

Time Allocation: 15% of Session Time

The Scenario Setup

You need to update your Senior Vice President on a critical project status. They have just 90 seconds before their next meeting. The project is facing an unexpected technical delay, but you've identified a potential workaround. Your objective is to inform them of the delay, present the solution, and request their approval to proceed with the workaround.

The Input Baseline (The Fallback)

Okay, so, about Project Phoenix. We've been working really hard on the integration phase, and things were going well, but then last Tuesday, during the stress tests, we hit a snag with the API. It's a pretty complex issue with the data migration module. We've had the team looking at it since then, and we've explored a few options. Initially, we thought about rolling back, but that would set us back two weeks. Then Sarah in engineering, she's really sharp, suggested a temporary fix using a different library, which we've prototyped, and it looks promising. So, I wanted to see what you thought about that, because it means we can still hit our deadline, mostly.

Phase 3: Technical Demonstration

Time Allocation: 10% of Session Time

Rule 1

State the Bottom Line Up Front (BLUF): Begin immediately with the most critical information—the core problem, solution, or request. No preamble, no historical context.

Rule 2

Provide concise, quantified justification and next steps: Follow the BLUF with 1-2 sentences explaining 'why' or 'how' (impact, solution overview), and then state the immediate action required or recommended. Maintain a strict 60-second limit.

Coach Script (The Target Output)

VP, Project Phoenix has an unexpected 3-day technical delay due to an API integration error. We've developed a validated workaround that will keep us on schedule with minimal risk, avoiding a two-week setback. I need your approval to implement this workaround immediately.

Phase 4: Deliberate Practice & Peer Feedback Loops

Time Allocation: 60% of Session Time

The Global Speaker Bar Success Metrics — to pass, the student must flawlessly demonstrate all three tracking pillars simultaneously.

  1. Technical Accuracy: The update must begin with the core issue, solution, or request within the first 10 seconds. It must contain no more than three distinct points (BLUF, Justification, Action). The language must be precise, active voice, and entirely free of hedging or tentative phrases.
  2. Linguistic Fluency / Bimodal Alignment: Physical Stage: Project voice clearly, use deliberate hand gestures to emphasize key points (e.g., 'problem,' 'solution,' 'request'), maintain consistent eye contact across the audience. Virtual Frame: Maintain direct lens contact for 80% of the delivery. Ensure microphone resonance is strong and consistent, avoiding vocal fades. Frame should be stable, showing head and shoulders, with minimal distracting background movement.
  3. Executive Presence: Zero filler words (um, ah, like, you know). Steady, confident vocal resonance throughout. Maintain a composed, authoritative demeanor. Avoid any vocal uptalk or questioning tone unless posing a direct question. Conclude with a definitive statement or clear request, not a trailing off.

The Core Drill

The BLUF Update: Students will deliver a 60-second update based on a provided business scenario, strictly adhering to the BLUF structure. The update must conclude with a clear call to action or recommendation.

The Rotational Execution Guidelines

Students will work in pairs. One student acts as the Presenter, the other as the Executive (timekeeper and feedback provider). Each Presenter gets two attempts. First attempt is a raw delivery. Second attempt incorporates immediate peer feedback on structure, timing, and presence. Roles then reverse. Coach provides overarching feedback and validates passes/fails against the Global Speaker Bar. Physical: Presenter stands, maintains eye contact. Virtual: Presenter ensures direct lens contact, strong vocal projection into microphone, optimal frame.

Phase 5: The Complexity Shift (Conditional Trigger)

Stress-Test 1 (Time Crunch)

Deliver the same BLUF update in 30 seconds, including the call to action. You now have even less time, requiring even more aggressive conciseness.

Stress-Test 2 (Adversarial)

During your 60-second BLUF update, the 'Executive' interrupts after 15 seconds with a challenging, skeptical question ('Why wasn't this caught earlier?'). You must acknowledge the interruption, provide a concise, direct answer, and immediately pivot back to your BLUF structure without losing composure or exceeding the total time limit.
